## What Makes Halvar Different from Regular Hosting

Let's be real: most hosting companies claim to be "green" because they bought some carbon offset certificates and called it a day. Halvar goes a bit further.

Their servers run on 100% green electricity. The heat the servers produce goes on to warm an entire block of office buildings nearby. They use second-hand and recycled hardware — and no, that doesn't mean slow servers, the machines are still on NVMe storage and LiteSpeed. Part of their revenue goes to JustDiggit, an organisation that works on restoring degraded land in Africa. They're also verified by the Green Web Foundation.

The name "Halvar" comes from Old Norwegian — it means "defender of the rock." Which, if you squint at it from the right angle, is a pretty solid mission statement for a planet-focused hosting company.

## Who Is Halvar Hosting Actually For?

This is squarely aimed at developers and agencies managing multiple client sites. Their panel gives you one login for all sites, SSH access, rsync, git, wp-cli, composer, cron jobs — the full developer toolkit. It's not a spoon-fed shared host that locks you out of anything useful.

If you run a small personal blog and never want to touch a terminal, you'll still be fine here. But the real value shows up when you're juggling 3, 5, or 20 sites and want a single organised setup that doesn't cost a fortune.

## The Tech Specs Worth Mentioning

Every shared and reseller plan includes LiteSpeed web server — which is meaningfully faster than Apache for most PHP workloads. There's also an X-ray PHP tracer built in, which is a legitimately useful debugging tool that most budget hosts don't offer at all.

Server monitoring runs every 30 seconds. Backups are nightly and recoverable in a couple of clicks via JetBackup. PHP version switching is handled through an easy selector in the control panel.

The servers are located in the EU, which keeps things GDPR-compliant without any configuration on your end. Data is stored and owned by EU companies — not just hosted in EU data centres of US corporations.
